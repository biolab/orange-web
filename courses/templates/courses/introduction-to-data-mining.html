Introduction to Data Mining

Data science is an interdisciplinary field that deals with scientific methods, processes, and systems to extract knowledge or insights from data. The course will introduce machine learning and data visualization techniques to uncover hidden patterns in data.

Why should I attend?

  • You will learn essential approaches in data science that include data exploration, visualization, unsupervised machine learning, classification and regression.
  • After completing the course, you will be able to analyze your own data and use them to develop predictive models.
  • The course will be hands-on and will introduce data science concepts and methods through the analysis of real-life data from biomedicine.

What will I learn?

  • Explorative data analysis
  • Data visualization
  • Predictive models (classification and regression)
  • Clustering and segmentation
  • Data projection

Lecture outline and schedule

September 11, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Data exploration. Data preparation for machine learning, introduction to data analysis workflows, visual programming as a means of design of data analysis workflows, basic data exploration and visualization, rigor and reproducibility in data mining.

September 12, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Introduction to classification. Classification trees. Confusion matrix. Estimation of prediction error. Overfitting. Cross-validation.

September 13, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Classification models. Classification trees, forests, logistic regression, nearest neighbors classification. Data painting. Area under ROC curve. Analysis of results of classifications. Nomograms.

September 15, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Regression. Linear regression, polynomial regression and overfitting. Regularization. Effects of regularization on training and test sets. Random forests for regression.

September 19, 2015. 10:00-12:00. N315

Partitional clustering. Introduction to k-means clustering. Differences with hierarchical clustering. Silhouette scoring of clustering results. Outlier detection.

September 20, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Data projections. Principal component analysis. Multi-dimensional scaling and t-SNE.

September 22, 2017. 10:00-12:00. N315

Gentle introduction to deep learning. Image embedding. Image data analysis – examples with clustering, classification and outlier detection with images. Course wrap-up.
